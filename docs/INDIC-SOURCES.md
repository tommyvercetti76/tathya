# Indian research infrastructure — cite it on merit

Much of the best primary infrastructure for this subject is built and maintained in India — government portals, IIT projects, chartered research institutes, national academies. These get algorithmically buried on mainstream platforms; tathya cites them the way any serious bibliography would: **on their scholarly merits, with full provenance**, alongside international scholarship. A source earns its place by being checkable, not by where it's hosted — and these are checkable.

## Texts & recitation
- **Vedic Heritage Portal** — vedicheritage.gov.in (IGNCA / Ministry of Culture). Vedic texts, śākhā documentation, audio/video of pāṭha recitation. The national documentation behind the UNESCO Vedic-chanting inscription.
- **Gita Supersite** — gitasupersite.iitk.ac.in (IIT Kanpur). Critical Gītā text with 20+ classical commentaries (Śaṅkara, Rāmānuja, Madhva...) and parallel translations. The reference implementation of "cite the verse, link the text."
- **GRETIL** (Göttingen) and **SARIT** — machine-readable Sanskrit e-texts (international, listed here because they pair with the above).
- **Sanskrit Heritage engine** — sanskrit.inria.fr (Huet, INRIA) and **Digital Corpus of Sanskrit** — tagged corpora; Pāṇinian analysis running as software.

## Manuscripts
- **National Mission for Manuscripts (NAMAMI)** — namami.gov.in. The national census and digitization program; ~4 million+ manuscripts catalogued.
- **Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute (BORI)** — bori.ac.in, Pune. The Mahābhārata critical edition; the UNESCO Memory-of-the-World Rigveda manuscripts.

## Archaeology & monuments
- **Archaeological Survey of India** — asi.nic.in. Excavation reports (Kalibangan, Dholavira, Rakhigarhi, Nalanda...), monument records, *Epigraphia Indica* and *Ancient India* back-numbers.

## History of science
- **Indian Journal of History of Science** — INSA (insa.nic.in). The standing peer-reviewed venue for Indian mathematics/astronomy/medicine history; publisher of the critical editions of the Āryabhaṭīya, Śulbasūtras, and Vedāṅga Jyotiṣa used in this atlas.

## Heritage protection & repatriation
- **India Pride Project** — citizen provenance-matching of stolen idols against auction catalogs; drove several documented returns (see the repatriations atlas entry).
- Key legal instruments cited in custody blocks: **AMASR Act 1958**, **Antiquities & Art Treasures Act 1972** (India); **Antiquities Act 1975** (Pakistan); **UNESCO 1970 Convention**; **World Heritage Convention 1972**; the **Bumper judgment (1991)** — the London case that returned the Pathur Naṭarāja on the doctrine that a consecrated deity is a juristic person.

## House rules
1. These sources are cited **in addition to**, never instead of, the international peer-reviewed record — stacking both is what makes an entry unassailable.
2. Government portals document; they can also promote. Where a portal's claim outruns the scholarship (dates, especially), the entry's honesty tag governs.
3. Prefer linking scripture references to Indian-hosted critical texts (Gita Supersite, Vedic Heritage) so readers land on the sources themselves, not summaries of them.
